## Richard
**A person receiving care for multiple health conditions**

- Age: 55
- Gender: Male
- Employment: Part-time / retired
- Digital literacy: Hesitant with technology

### Jobs to be done
- Keep appointments organised and understandable, in one place, with clear next steps
- Track trends over time
- Successfully manage one condition in a way that is concordant with other conditions

### Key challenges
- Poor coordination between GPs, hospitals, and private healthcare providers
- Fragmented systems
- Repeatedly explaining medical history due to lack of integrated records

### Relevant app features
- Chronic Disease Management
- Waiting for Care
- Appointments
- Self-declared medicines
- Health data features (EU screens: Procedures, Conditions, Allergies)

### Research references
- IPPOSI focus groups summary
- Health data focus groups

## Shane
**A person with a disability and a chronic health condition to manage**

- Age: 33
- Gender: Male
- Digital literacy: Competent user of assistive technology. Low tolerance for apps that break accessibility standards. Keen supporter of digitalised support in healthcare for efficiency.

### Jobs to be done
- Log in and use the HSE Health App independently
- Understand additional supports available prior to appointments
- Advocate for his needs, particularly in public healthcare settings

### Key challenges
- Accessibility breakdowns such as unstructured content
- Needs support with MyGovID login, particularly 2FA

### Relevant app features
- Accessibility Testing / DPO Engagements
- Waiting for Care
- Chronic Disease Management
- Self-declared Medicines
- Health data features (EU screens: Procedures, Conditions, Allergies)

### Research references
- Disabled Persons Organisation Consultation (DPO) summary
- HSE Health App potential to improve collaboration between disabled individuals and healthcare practitioners

## Brian
**Clinical Companion**

- Age: 30
- Gender: Male
- Role: Outpatient patient support worker (PSW)
- Digital literacy: Confident with everyday apps. Low tolerance for overly complex or slow systems when delivering care.

### Jobs to be done
- Help patients find appointments, sign up, and manage care pathways
- Support patient self-sufficiency by instructing them on how to use features themselves
- Report activity to management

### Key challenges
- Varying digital literacy of patients
- Unique challenges of supporting patients over the phone

### Relevant app features
- Prostate Cancer Care Feature
- Appointments

### Research references
- Hospital staff would highly value multilingual content to overcome language barriers and improve patient care
- Users with literacy and language barriers heavily rely on primary healthcare workers to access care
